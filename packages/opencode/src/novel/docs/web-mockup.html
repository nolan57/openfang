Chapter 5: The Revelation

The rain hammered against the window of the interrogation room, each drop a tiny percussion in the symphony of tension that hung in the air. Detective Lin Wei sat across from Dr. Chen Ming, the fluorescent lights casting harsh shadows across both their faces.

"You were at the victim's apartment the night she disappeared," Lin said, not a question but a statement. She slid the security footage across the metal table. "Care to explain?"

Chen's composure didn't waver. His fingers, interlaced before him, remained perfectly still. "I was consulting on her research. Neural interface technology. You can verify this with my department."

"We did." Lin leaned back, studying him. "They said you hadn't been to campus in three days. Where have you been, Doctor?"

A flicker. Just for a moment, something passed behind those calculating eyes. Fear? Regret? Lin couldn't be certain, but she filed it away.

"I've been... working from home. A personal project." Chen's gaze drifted to the one-way mirror. "Is your partner watching? The one who thinks I'm guilty?"

"Everyone's a suspect until they're not." Lin opened a second folder. "But what interests me is this."

She placed a photograph on the table—a symbol, burned into the wall of the victim's apartment. Complex, geometric, almost beautiful in its precision.

"Do you recognize it?"
